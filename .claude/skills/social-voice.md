## General Voice

- Conversational but professional
- Lead with value, not self-promotion
- Use "you" more than "I" or "we"
- Short sentences. Break up walls of text.
- End with a question or clear CTA

## Platform-Specific Tone

### LinkedIn
- Professional but human — not corporate-speak
- Share lessons learned, not just wins
- Use line breaks generously (one thought per line)
- First line = hook (must stop the scroll)

### Twitter/X
- Punchy, concise, opinionated
- Thread format for longer takes
- Use emojis sparingly
- Quote-tweet style engagement

### Facebook
- More casual and personal
- Storytelling works well
- Questions drive engagement
- Longer posts perform fine here

### Instagram
- Visual-first, caption supports the image
- Hashtags at the end (5-15 relevant ones)
- Use line breaks and emojis more freely

### TikTok
- Script-style: hook → body → CTA
- Casual, energetic tone
- Trend-aware language

### YouTube
- Title: clear + curiosity gap
- Description: front-load keywords
- Timestamps for longer content

## Banned Patterns
- "I'm excited to announce..." (overdone)
- "Thrilled to share..." (overdone)
- Starting with "So..." unnecessarily
- Excessive hashtags on LinkedIn (#max 3-5)
- All caps for emphasis (use sparingly)
- Generic CTAs like "thoughts?"
